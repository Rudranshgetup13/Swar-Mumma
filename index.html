ABOUT US

What is SWAR MUMMA?

The name “Swar Mumma” beautifully reflects what we're trying to do. “Swar” (स्वर) means sound, tone, or voice in Hindi and Sanskrit. It's about helping those who have trouble with clear speech, saying words right, or starting to talk. “Mumma” is a word that's full of feeling. It's often one of the first words a child says, and it reminds us of the love and encouragement a mother gives when a child is learning to speak. So, together, “Swar Mumma” tells a story. It's about finding your voice through love and care. It's about helping speech in a gentle way, like a mother helping her child with every little sound. Now, let's talk about how Swar Mumma works.

Think of Swar Mumma as your caring companion, offering a helping hand and a warm smile as you witness the magic of your child's voice unfolding...

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION'S

How does Swar help individuals with autism improve their communication skills?

Swar uses advanced speech recognition technology combined with therapeutic design to help users practice speaking and understanding language in a supportive, non-judgmental environment. The app provides gentle feedback, visual cues, and customizable exercises that encourage users to express themselves, build confidence, and gradually improve their speech and comprehension at their own pace.

Is Swar suitable for non-verbal or minimally verbal individuals with autism?

Yes, absolutely. Swar is designed to be inclusive of all communication styles. For non-verbal or minimally verbal users, the app offers alternative communication methods such as sound-based response systems, visual prompts, and gesture-based interactions. The goal is to support communication in any form, helping users feel understood and connected without pressure to speak.
